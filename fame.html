The Fame

  • The Good News: Edwards Air Force Base has made my family proud and famous.
  • The Bad News: A family member apparently has to crash and burn to become famous.

The Man

  • Family Relationship: Captain Glen Walter Edwards was my great-uncle.
  • Education:
    • Bachelor of Science: Major Chemical Engineering from UC-Berkley
    • Master of Science: Major Aeronautical Sciences from Princeton
  • Military Career:
    • Enlisted: Air Force Cadet (5 months after Pearl Harbor)
    • Medals: 4 Distingused Flying Cross Medals (+ 6 other awards)
    • Stationed: North Africa for most of WWII
    • Test Pilot: Spent most of post-war career as test pilot
  • Final Flight:
    • 5 June 1948
    • Test piloting Northrup YB-49 Bomber
    • Plane broke apart mid-air
    • All five crew members died

The Plane

  • Family: The YB-49 is part of a family of aircraft, known as "flying wings," because they have no tail or separate fuselage.
  • Handling: Unimpressed by the plane's handling, Captain Edwards wrote in his diary that the YB-49 was "the dardest airplne I've ever tried to do anyting with. Quite uncontrollable at times."
  • Great-Grand-Child The B-2 Stealth Bomber, still in service, is a descendent of (and made possible by) the YB-49 test flights.

The Base

  • Name: Located in the middle of the Mohave Desert, Edwards Air Force Base was initially called "Mohave Field" and was known as "Muroc Airforce Base" when Captain Edwards' plane crashed."
  • Recent Fame: In recent years, Edwards Air Force Base became well-known as the main landing site for the Space Shuttle.
  • Role Almost every U.S. military aircraft since the 1950s has been tested at Edwards, the site of many aviation breakthroughs.
